About My Pet Footprint

My Pet Footprint is an animated climate comedy series that uses humor to expose the fossil fuel industry's role in creating climate guilt while avoiding responsibility.

Think "Don't Look Up" meets "Rick and Morty" - we're creating biting satire that makes you laugh while making you think.

Presented by Greenpeace, this independent series aims to shift the climate conversation from individual guilt to corporate accountability.

Why This Matters

The fossil fuel industry has spent decades shifting blame to individuals while avoiding accountability

Their Story

It's Your Carbon Footprint

The term "carbon footprint" was popularized by BP in a $100 million campaign to shift responsibility to individuals.

Plastic Recycling Myth

Oil companies promoted plastic recycling while knowing most plastic cannot be recycled effectively.

Greenwashing Campaigns

Fossil fuel companies spend millions on ads about their "green initiatives" while expanding fossil fuel production.

The Reality

Corporate Emissions

Just 100 companies are responsible for 71% of global emissions since 1988, according to the Carbon Majors Report.

Decades of Deception

Internal documents show oil companies knew about climate change since the 1970s but funded denial campaigns.

Policy Obstruction

The fossil fuel industry spends over $200 million annually lobbying against climate policies in the US alone.

Our comedy series aims to expose these tactics through humor and help shift the conversation back to corporate accountability

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our climate comedy project

My Pet Footprint is an animated comedy series that satirizes the fossil fuel industry's role in creating climate guilt while avoiding responsibility. It's a Greenpeace-presented project that uses humor to shift the climate conversation from individual blame to corporate accountability.

Your donation will directly fund the production of the animated series, including artist salaries, animation costs, voice acting, sound design, and distribution. We're committed to transparency and will provide regular updates on how funds are being used throughout the production process.

We aim to release the pilot episode within 6 months of reaching our funding goal. The full season will follow approximately 8-10 months after that. Donors will get early access to episodes before public release.

Yes, as this project is presented by Greenpeace, a registered non-profit organization, your donation is tax-deductible to the extent allowed by law. You will receive a receipt for your donation that can be used for tax purposes.

There are many ways to support the project! You can share our campaign on social media, join our community forums to provide feedback on early concepts, volunteer your skills if you're an artist or animator, or simply help spread the word about the project's mission.
